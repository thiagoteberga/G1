5 Management Tools for Startups

You may not even know it, but you sure need management tools for your startup. Thousands of chores to do and you get lost between marketing, managing sales goals and team performance; make your product run with the best usability... and in the middle of it you still have to grow and keep alive the spirit of startup.

This is a common scenario among startups. Working within large uncertainties, having a lean team, and yet having to take care of all your company's needs on a tight budget, is not an easy task. Managing a startup means doing more with less - as well as learning that you can not do it all on your own. Check out tips to uncomplicate your startup management experience here.

To build and expand your startup you need to invest in tools that help improve each of your work areas by automating and optimizing everything from customer support to software development. Based on reviews and prices, we have organized the list of the best management tools for startups:

Best Management Tools for Startups (2017):

We divided the list into the top 5 management tools categories, taking into consideration the major startups’ pains:

  • Process and Task Management;
  • Business Intelligence and Data Analysis;
  • Customer support and Collaboration.

Process and Task Management

Pipefy

Pipefy is on the top of the list because of its flexibility (and price) that make it a great tool for any type of startup to manage its workflow processes.

By leveraging process management and automation, teams can accomplish much more in half the time.

Using best prebuilt market practice models , such as the product roadmap or Onboarding of new employees, you can start managing your processes right away.

The highly customizable and customizable Pipefy software is free for up to 10 users and paid plans start at $9 a month by user.

Client Support

Front

Front allows teams to centralize all their different communication channels into one place. Team members can assign and send private messages, better manage shared email addresses, and configure rule-based automation to increase productivity.



You can try Front 14-free trial and after the basic plan starts at $15 per user / month when billed annually.

Business Intelligence and Data Analysis

Mixpanel

Mixpanel is the right tool to help you learn about how people use your product. It offers advanced mobile and web analytics tools as well as funnel views to help you analyze your users' behavior. Offers free plan for up to 25k data points and paid plans starting at $150 a month.

CoBlue

CoBlue is the software to help you set goals that aim adding global value to your business. Create qualitative goals and measure their success with quantitative goals, using the OKR methodology. Coblue's plan starts at $18 per user / monthly with a minimum of 10 users.

Colaboration

Appear.in

Appear.in allows you to easily connect with you team members. You can create a link from a chat room and share it with anyone you need to get in touch - no registration is required.

The platform allows you to create a link from the chat room for free and you can upgrade it for $12 a month per room to gain access to the special features.

We hope at least one of these tools, or several of them, will be helpful resources to make your startup grow. Share with your team and your network.
